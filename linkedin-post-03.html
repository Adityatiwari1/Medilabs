Not all lab glass is created equal.

Use the wrong type, and your experiment could shatter — literally.

Many labs still use soda-lime glass to cut costs. But soda-lime glass expands significantly when heated, making it prone to cracking during thermal changes.

That's why top research labs worldwide have switched to borosilicate glass.

Here's why borosilicate glass dominates the lab:

  • Low thermal expansion — resists cracking during rapid heating and cooling
  • Chemical resistance — withstands acids, alkalis, and organic solvents
  • Optical clarity — transparent enough for precise visual observation
  • Mechanical strength — more durable than standard glass under daily lab stress
  • Autoclave safe — can be sterilized at high temperatures repeatedly
At Medilab Exports, 100% of our glassware is made from borosilicate glass (3.3 coefficient of expansion). We don't compromise — because your research shouldn't have to either.
