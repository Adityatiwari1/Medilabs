You're probably damaging your lab glassware without knowing it.

These 5 myths are responsible for most premature breakage in laboratories.

Lab professionals are trained in science — but rarely in glassware care.

The result? Expensive equipment fails earlier than it should, and contamination risks increase silently.

Let's bust these 5 glassware maintenance myths:

  • Myth 1: "Any detergent works for lab glassware" — Wrong. Household detergents leave residues. Use lab-grade cleaners only
  • Myth 2: "Air drying is fine" — Tap water spots can contain minerals that contaminate future experiments
  • Myth 3: "Borosilicate glass is unbreakable" — It's heat-resistant, not indestructible. Thermal shock can still crack it
  • Myth 4: "Scratched glassware is still usable" — Scratches create stress points that lead to breakage and inaccurate readings
  • Myth 5: "Autoclaving replaces chemical cleaning" — Autoclaving sterilizes, but doesn't remove chemical residues
