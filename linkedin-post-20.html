Most labs don't audit their glassware until something breaks.

By then, you've already wasted time, money, and possibly compromised results.

A quarterly glassware audit takes 2 hours. Recovering from a failed experiment due to damaged glassware takes weeks.

Here's a simple checklist every lab manager should follow.

Your Laboratory Glassware Audit Checklist:

  • ✅ Count all inventory — compare against your purchasing records
  • ✅ Inspect for damage — check for chips, cracks, scratches, and clouding
  • ✅ Verify calibration marks — ensure graduation lines are legible and accurate
  • ✅ Check stopper and joint fit — worn ground glass joints cause leaks and contamination
  • ✅ Assess cleaning condition — residue buildup indicates improper cleaning protocols
  • ✅ Identify missing items — create a reorder list for commonly used pieces
  • ✅ Document everything — log findings, disposals, and replacement orders
