The Erlenmeyer flask was designed in 1860.

166 years later, nobody has improved the shape. Here's why.

German chemist Emil Erlenmeyer didn't design the conical flask for aesthetics. Every angle, every curve serves a specific scientific purpose.

It's one of the most elegant examples of form following function in science.

The genius of the conical design — 5 engineering advantages:

  • Wide base — prevents tipping over during vigorous mixing
  • Narrow neck — reduces splashing and limits evaporation of volatile solvents
  • Conical walls — allow easy swirling without spilling liquid
  • Easy to seal — the narrow opening fits stoppers and cotton plugs perfectly
  • Versatile — works for titrations, cultures, storage, heating, and mixing
