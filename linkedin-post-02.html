Ever wondered what happens BEFORE a beaker reaches your lab bench?

The manufacturing process is far more complex than most scientists realize.

Every piece of lab glassware goes through a precise, multi-step process to ensure it can withstand extreme temperatures, chemicals, and daily wear.

A single flaw at any stage can compromise an entire experiment.

Here's how scientific glassware is made — step by step:

  • Raw Material Selection — high-purity borosilicate glass tubes and rods
  • Flame Shaping — skilled glassblowers shape each piece using precision burners
  • Annealing — controlled cooling in ovens to relieve internal stress
  • Calibration & Marking — graduation marks are etched for measurement accuracy
  • Quality Inspection — every piece is checked for defects, dimensions, and tolerances
  • Packaging — secure, export-grade packing to prevent damage during shipping
At Medilab Exports, our factory follows each of these steps with strict quality control at every stage. We manufacture glassware that meets international standards — and ship it safely to labs in over 35 countries.
